## 1. Purpose

V1.5 authority rules define who can perform project, task, gate, readiness, reporting, and acceptance actions.

---

## 2. Core authority principle

V1.5 authority should remain role-based and action-bounded.
Management authority must expand carefully for project-first PMO behavior without weakening delivery-role boundaries.

---

## 3. V1.5 authority layers

### A. Governance Layer
Includes:
- Alex
- Nova

Can:
- make final governance decisions
- approve/reject formal completion
- review Maverick and Jarvis outputs
- override recommendations

### B. Management / PMO Layer
Includes:
- Jarvis
- Maverick

Can:
- create/update project
- create/update/close task when within PMO authority
- assign ownership
- coordinate known agents internally (Maverick)
- prepare reports
- prepare acceptance packages
- return coordination status to PMO surface
- make management-level operational moves within policy

### C. Delivery Layer
Includes:
- BA
- SA
- DEV
- QA

Can:
- update owned task state
- record/resolve blockers for owned work
- submit handoff
- provide delivery artifacts and stage outputs

---

## 4. Object/action authority rules

### Project-level authority
- `create_project` -> Governance + authorized PMO layer
- `update_project` -> Governance + authorized PMO layer
- `close_project` -> Governance layer final authority

### Task-level authority
- `create_task` -> authorized PMO layer
- `update_task` -> authorized PMO layer and current owner where appropriate
- `assign_owner` -> authorized PMO layer
- `close_task` -> PMO layer or Governance layer as defined by policy

### Task-state authority
- `update_task_state` -> current owner + authorized PMO layer
- `record_blocker` -> current owner + authorized PMO layer
- `resolve_blocker` -> current owner + authorized PMO layer

### Handoff authority
- `submit_handoff` -> current owner or authorized PMO layer acting explicitly

### Gate authority
- `approve/reject/hold/stop/return` -> Governance layer or explicitly delegated gate authority only

### Intake authority (human-governed)
- Human (Alex/Jarvis) makes go/no-go decision at intake — not automated
- PMO enforces kickoff blocked without active project selection (form-level)
- No automated ReadinessCheck subsystem in V1.5

### Reporting authority
- reports may be generated by PMO layer
- reports do not create governance truth; they summarize Platform Core truth

### Acceptance authority
- acceptance package can be prepared by PMO / delivery contributors
- final acceptance decision remains Governance-layer controlled

---

## 5. Maverick position in V1.5

Maverick is no longer only a logical placeholder.
In V1.5, Maverick becomes an explicit internal coordination layer actor.

However:
- Maverick coordinates only — humans (Alex/Jarvis) make decisions at intake/gates
- Maverick must not become autonomous decision-maker
- Maverick must not become the hidden system of record
- Maverick advisory output must not silently mutate governed truth

---

## 6. One-line frozen definition

**V1.5 authority remains role-based and action-bounded: Governance Layer retains final decision authority, Management/PMO Layer coordinates internally and returns status, human operators decide at intake and gates, Delivery Layer operates owned execution work, and Maverick coordinates known agents internally without becoming autonomous decision-maker or hidden source of truth.****
